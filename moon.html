Neuroscience of Perception

The human brain processes 11 million bits of information per second but consciously attends to only about 40-50 bits. What determines which information rises to consciousness? Resonance—alignment with existing neural pathways, emotional states, and cognitive patterns.

When content resonates with our inner frameworks (truth, curiosity, wonder), it enhances understanding. When it doesn't resonate, it becomes noise. This explains both individual perception and collective attention patterns in media.
